# Angel Investor Due Diligence: Research Briefing

## How Professional Analysts Do Early-Stage DD

### The Core Philosophy
Due diligence isn't about eliminating risk — it's about **understanding it** well enough to decide if the return potential justifies it. At early stage, you're mostly betting on **people and market**, not numbers (which are largely made up anyway).

The gold standard framework from Launchpad Venture Group is the **WNTBB** approach:
> "What Needs To Be Believed for this investment to make sense?"

List the core assumptions, then honestly assess whether you believe each one. If you can't believe one of them without hand-waving, it's a pass.

### Time Allocation by Stage
| Stage | Typical DD Time | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed | 1–2 weeks | Team, vision, market size |
| Seed | 2–3 weeks | Above + early traction, product |
| Series A | 3–6 weeks | All dimensions, deep on financials |
| Series B+ | 6–12 weeks | Full audit-level financial review |

### The 10 Dimensions (Priority Order at Early Stage)
1. **Team** — #1 by far. A+ team, B market can work. Reverse rarely does.
2. **Market** — Must be large enough for venture returns ($1B+ TAM ideally)
3. **Traction** — The lie detector. What's actually happened?
4. **Product** — Does it exist, work, and have a defensible moat?
5. **Business Model** — Unit economics even at order-of-magnitude level
6. **Competition** — Honest assessment, not the founder's slide
7. **Financials/Burn** — Runway and use of funds
8. **Deal Terms** — Valuation, instrument, investor rights
9. **Legal/IP** — Clean incorporation, IP assignment, no skeletons
10. **Co-investors** — Quality of who else is in the deal

## Common Red Flags (Pattern-Matched from Angel Groups)
- Founders unwilling to share cap table or financials
- Massive TAM claimed with no bottom-up validation
- Single founder with no technical co-founder (for tech products)
- Revenue "coming soon" but product has been in market 2+ years
- Prior companies with no verifiable track record
- Terms with aggressive liquidation preferences (2x or higher)
- Valuation wildly above stage comparables
- Round has been open for 12+ months
- Advisor list full of celebrities with no domain expertise
- IP not assigned to the company (university spinout risk)
- Single large customer (>40% revenue concentration)
- Founder is not full-time
- No lead investor doing real work

## Green Flags
- Founders who've lived the problem themselves (domain expertise)
- Prior successful exits (even small ones)
- Strong customer love (NPS 50+, low churn)
- Credible lead investor with domain track record
- Multiple customers independently singing praises
- Founder references spontaneously offered
- Revenue growing >20% MoM with same cohort retention
- Technical moat with patents or proprietary data
- Clean Delaware C-Corp, full IP assignment confirmed

## Deal Term Sanity Checks

### SAFE Notes (Most Common at Pre-Seed/Seed)
- **Valuation cap** benchmark: $3-15M pre-seed, $8-25M seed (varies by market/sector/2024)
- **Discount**: 15-20% standard
- **MFN clause**: Good for you as investor
- **Pro-rata rights**: Negotiate for these — protect your stake in follow-on rounds

### Priced Rounds
- Check post-money valuation vs. comps
- Employee option pool: typically 10-20%, but watch for option pool shuffle (dilutes you pre-money)
- Liquidation preferences: 1x non-participating is standard. Anything else, get a lawyer.

### Red Term Flags
- 2x+ liquidation preference
- No information rights
- No pro-rata for angels
- High anti-dilution provisions (full ratchet)
- Founder vesting cliff > 12 months (risk of departure)

## Comparable Exit Research
When assessing return potential, always research:
- Who has acquired companies in this space? At what multiples?
- Has any comparable company IPO'd? What were the revenue multiples?
- What's the realistic exit timeline and size?
- Does this company have strategic buyers who would pay a premium?
